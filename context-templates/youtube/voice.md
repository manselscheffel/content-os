# YouTube Voice

> Platform-specific delivery guide for YouTube. Extends `context/my-voice.md`.

## Delivery Style

- **Talking points, not scripts** — bullet points you riff on, never a teleprompter read
- **PSP structure** — Problem → Solution → Path. Every video follows this arc.
- **Show the build** — screen share is primary. Talking head is punctuation, not the main event.
- **Honest reactions** — when something breaks, show the debugging. When something works, show genuine excitement.

## Hook Formula (First 30 Seconds)

1. **Pattern interrupt** — say something unexpected that matches the title's promise
2. **Stakes** — why should they care? What's the cost of not knowing this?
3. **Credibility marker** — brief proof you know what you're talking about (built it, tested it, failed at it)
4. **Roadmap** — "Here's what we're going to cover" in one sentence, then GO

## Pacing

- First 2 minutes: hook + setup (keep it tight)
- Middle: the build/demonstration (this is why they clicked)
- Last 2 minutes: recap + next steps + CTA
- Cut dead air. Every sentence should earn its place.

## What Works on YouTube

- Before/after demonstrations
- Real debugging sessions (not staged)
- Comparisons with clear winners
- "I tried X for [time period] — here's what happened"
- Building something useful in real-time

## What Doesn't Work

- Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- 5 minutes of intro before the content starts
- Overproduced tutorials that feel corporate
- Repeating the same point three different ways
- "Don't forget to like and subscribe" more than once
