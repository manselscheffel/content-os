# LinkedIn Post Frameworks

> Category-specific writing frameworks. Each framework describes the natural flow — the examples in `examples.md` show what it actually looks like.

---

## Contrarian

**Purpose**: Challenge conventional wisdom with proof.

**Flow**:
1. **Bold claim** that goes against what everyone's saying
2. **The reason** most people get it wrong (empathy, not attack)
3. **Your proof** — what you actually did/saw/built that proves it
4. **The twist** — what's actually true instead
5. **Land it** — one punchy sentence that sticks

**Tone**: Confident but not arrogant. You're sharing what you learned, not lecturing.

---

## Video Drop

**Purpose**: Announce a YouTube video with a standalone insight.

**Flow**:
1. **Insight hook** — the most surprising thing from the video (NOT "I just posted a new video")
2. **Context** — why this matters right now
3. **Tease** — what you cover that nobody else does
4. **Link** — natural transition to the video URL

**Tone**: Excited but not hype-y. The insight sells the click, not the announcement.

---

## PSP (Problem-Solution-Path)

**Purpose**: Teach a framework or system.

**Flow**:
1. **Problem** — specific pain your audience feels (make them nod)
2. **Solution** — your approach in one sentence
3. **Path** — 3-5 steps, each in 1-2 lines
4. **Result** — what happens when they follow the path

**Tone**: Practical and direct. You're giving them a map, not a lecture.

---

## New Tool

**Purpose**: Review or showcase a tool/workflow.

**Flow**:
1. **What it does** in one sentence (no jargon)
2. **Why you tried it** — the problem it solved for you
3. **Honest verdict** — what worked, what didn't
4. **Who it's for** — specific use case, not everyone

**Tone**: Honest reviewer. Not sponsored, not a shill.

---

## Motivation

**Purpose**: Vulnerable admission or lesson learned.

**Flow**:
1. **The moment** — a specific scene, not a vague feeling
2. **The lesson** — what you learned (not what you already knew)
3. **The application** — how it changed what you do now

**Tone**: Real. Not motivational poster. Not fishing for sympathy.

---

## Story

**Purpose**: Build log, behind-the-scenes, personal experience.

**Flow**:
1. **Set the scene** — time, place, stakes
2. **The challenge** — what went wrong or what you were up against
3. **What you did** — specific actions, decisions, pivots
4. **The outcome** — honest result, good or bad

**Tone**: Narrator. You're telling a story, not teaching a lesson. The lesson emerges from the story.

---

## List

**Purpose**: Numbered items, tips, predictions, curated picks.

**Flow**:
1. **Provocative framing** — why this list matters
2. **Items** — 3-7 items, each with a punchy headline and one line of context
3. **Closer** — the pattern or takeaway that ties the list together

**Tone**: Curated and opinionated. Each item is a mini-opinion, not a neutral fact.
