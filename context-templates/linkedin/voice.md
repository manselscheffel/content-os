# LinkedIn Voice

> Platform-specific writing guide for LinkedIn. Extends `context/my-voice.md`.

## Format Rules

- **Hook-Twist-Pitch** structure on every post
- First line is the hook — must stop the scroll
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- Generous line breaks for mobile readability
- 400-850 characters optimal. Past 900 means you're over-explaining.
- No emojis in the opening line
- No hashtags in the body text — hashtags go at the very end

## Post Structure

**Hook** (line 1): Bold claim, question, or pattern interrupt. This is what shows in the feed preview.

**Twist** (body): The unexpected angle, proof, or story that makes the hook land. This is where your voice shines — funny, honest, specific.

**Pitch** (close): Soft CTA or landing thought. Not "follow me for more." Something that earns the follow: a question, a takeaway, a mic drop.

## What Works on LinkedIn

- Contrarian takes with proof
- Personal stories with a business lesson
- "Here's what I actually did" > "Here's what you should do"
- Absurd analogies that make complex things simple
- Admitting failure before showing the solution

## What Doesn't Work

- Corporate speak
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- Posts that start with "I'm excited to announce..."
- Listicles without personality
- Anything you wouldn't say out loud to a friend
