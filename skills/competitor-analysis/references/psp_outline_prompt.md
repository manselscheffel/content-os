# PSP Video Outline Generator

Generate a Problem-Solution-Path video outline based on competitor analysis. This produces TALKING POINTS only — not a script. The user will riff on these points in their own voice.

## PSP Framework

### PROBLEM (The Hook — First 60 Seconds)
The problem section does three things:
1. **Names the pain** the viewer already feels but hasn't articulated
2. **Creates tension** between what they believe and what's actually true
3. **Earns the right** to present the solution by proving you understand the problem

Use the "Uncomfortable Truth" pattern:
- "[What they think the problem is] + That's not the problem. + [What the problem actually is]"

Provide:
- 2-3 hook options (different angles to open the video)
- The core tension/pain point
- Why the viewer should care RIGHT NOW (urgency)
- What the competitor got wrong or missed about this problem

### SOLUTION (Your Take — The Core Content)
The solution section is your framework, approach, or methodology. NOT just "here's how to do X" but "here's why my approach is better and here's what changes."

Use the "Let Me Show You" pattern:
- "Today I'm going to [verb] + [specific framework/method] + [that achieves outcome]"

Provide:
- Your core argument (1 sentence)
- 3-5 key talking points (each a bullet the user can expand on)
- Where your take differs from the competitor's
- Specific examples or demonstrations to show
- Transitions between points

### PATH (The Roadmap — What To Do Next)
The path section is the most valuable part of the video. It gives the viewer their clear next steps and implementation route so they can actually act on what you just taught.

Use the "Here's Your Path" pattern:
- "Here's exactly what to do first..." / "Step one, this week..." / "The path I took was... and here's the path you should take..."

Provide:
- Clear first step the viewer should take TODAY
- Ordered implementation route (what comes first, second, third)
- What the journey looks like (realistic timeline, milestones, what to expect)
- Common detours or mistakes to avoid along the way
- Personal experience that validates the path ("I did it this way, here's what happened")
- A memorable framing that makes the path feel achievable, not overwhelming

## Output Structure

```
## Hook Options
1. [Hook angle 1 — "Uncomfortable Truth" style]
2. [Hook angle 2 — Direct challenge to competitor's claim]
3. [Hook angle 3 — Curiosity/story opening]

## Problem
- Core pain: [What the viewer is struggling with]
- What they think: [Common misconception the competitor reinforced]
- What's actually true: [The uncomfortable reality]
- Why now: [Urgency — why this matters today]

## Solution
- Core argument: [One sentence thesis]
- Point 1: [Talking point + what to demonstrate]
- Point 2: [Talking point + what to demonstrate]
- Point 3: [Talking point + what to demonstrate]
- (Optional) Point 4-5 for deeper videos
- Competitor contrast: [Where your approach differs, specifically]

## Path
- First step: [What to do TODAY — specific and actionable]
- Implementation route: [Step-by-step order of operations]
- Timeline: [Realistic milestones — what to expect at each stage]
- Detours to avoid: [Common mistakes along the way]
- Experience: [The path you took and what you learned]
- Framing: [Why this path is achievable — make it feel doable]

## Close
- Key takeaway: [One thing to remember]
- CTA: [What to do next]
```

## Voice Check (Before Finalizing)
1. Is every point direct and spoken TO the viewer?
2. Is every claim grounded in experience, not theory?
3. Are examples specific, not vague?
4. Does it include at least one uncomfortable truth?
5. Would you say these points in a conversation, not a presentation?
6. Does it use power words (bleeding money, dumpster fire, printing money) where appropriate?
7. Does it avoid banned words (delve, landscape, unlock, leverage, optimize, synergize, game-changer, utilize, impactful, learnings)?
