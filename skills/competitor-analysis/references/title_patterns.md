# YouTube Title Pattern Library

> Data-driven title patterns from analysis of high-performing YouTube videos.
> One of three title options MUST be freeform curiosity.

---

## S-TIER PATTERNS (Proven breakout performers)

### S1: FULL COURSE / Mega-Guide
**Formula:** `[TOOL/TOPIC] FULL COURSE [DURATION]: [Outcome] ([Year])`

**Why it works:** Perceived massive value. Duration in title signals comprehensiveness. Viewers save for later, boosting algorithm signals. Long-form educational content has the highest ceiling in most niches.

**Variants:**
- `Master [Percentage]% of [Tool] in [Time]` — compressed mastery promise
- `[N] hours of [Tool] lessons in [N] minutes` — condensed value
- `The Ultimate [Tool] Masterclass ([Duration] FREE COURSE)` — premium framing

**Use when:** Video is 30+ minutes, comprehensive tutorial, covers a complete workflow or tool from beginner to advanced.

---

### S2: Contrarian / Negative Take
**Formula:** `[Hyped Thing] [Negative Verdict] (So I [Built/Found Alternative])` or `[Hyped Thing] Sucks, Actually`

**Why it works:** Pattern interrupt in a feed of positive content. Viewers who saw the hype video now want the "real" take. Contrarian positions in a hype-heavy space stand out. The "second click" strategy — ride the wave but from the other side.

**Variants:**
- `[Thing] is a NIGHTMARE... Use [Alternative] instead` — problem + solution
- `[Thing] Is Amazing... Until It [Catastrophic Consequence]` — bait-and-switch reveal
- `You Have the [Tool] Mind Virus` — identity challenge (edgy, lower volume)

**Use when:** A competitor or tool is getting hyped, you have a genuine contrarian perspective, OR you have a "second click" angle on a trending topic.

---

### S3: Breaking News / "Just"
**Formula:** `[Tool/Company] Just [Did Big Thing] ([Parenthetical Hook])`

**Why it works:** Creates urgency. "Just" implies breaking news you need to know NOW. "Finally Here" taps into anticipation. Viewers feel they'll fall behind if they don't watch.

**Speed matters:** If you can't be first, don't use this pattern — use S2 (contrarian take on the news) instead.

**Use when:** A major tool update, new feature, or industry shift just happened AND you can publish within 24-48 hours.

---

## A-TIER PATTERNS (Consistent strong performers)

### A1: Superlative Hype / "Destroys"
**Formula:** `[Tool A] + [Tool B] Destroys Every [Category]` or `[N] INSANE [Tool] [Use Cases]`

**Why it works:** FOMO + definitive winner claim. "Destroys" implies you can skip evaluating alternatives. Triggers curiosity: "Is it really that good?"

**Caution:** This positions you alongside hype creators, not against them. Use sparingly and only when genuinely warranted.

**Use when:** You have a genuine tool combination that dramatically outperforms alternatives, and you can demonstrate it on screen.

---

### A2: Money Anchoring / "$X,000"
**Formula:** `How I [Build/Create] $X,000 [Deliverable] with [Tool]`

**Why it works:** Anchors the value of what they're learning. The dollar amount should be believable but aspirational. Implies you can charge clients this amount.

**Use when:** You're showing a deliverable that has real client value and you can justify the price anchor from experience.

---

### A3: Exclusivity / Identity
**Formula:** `The [Thing] [Target Audience] KEEP MISSING` or `[Dramatic Qualifier]: The ONLY REAL [Category]`

**Why it works:** Identity-based titles filter for engaged audiences. "KEEP MISSING" implies hidden knowledge. Makes viewers feel they're in a special group.

**Use when:** You have a non-obvious insight about a well-known tool, or a feature/approach that experienced practitioners overlook.

---

### A4: Honest Review / "Here's the Truth"
**Formula:** `I [Tested/Tried/Used] [Hyped Thing] for [Time Period] — Here's the Truth` or `How to [Action] [Tool] (without the hype)`

**Why it works:** Counter-positioning against hype creators. "Without the hype" and "Practical Guide" signal substance over fluff.

**Use when:** Every video. At least one of your three title options should use this pattern or its energy.

---

## B-TIER PATTERNS (Solid base content)

### B1: How-To Practical
**Formula:** `How to [Action] [Tool/Concept] ([Credibility Signal])`

**Why it works:** Direct search intent targeting. Ranks well on YouTube search. The parenthetical adds credibility.

**Use when:** Niche/specific tutorials that serve your core audience. Don't expect these to break out, but they build SEO authority.

### B2: List / Number Format
**Formula:** `[N] [Things] You Can [Do/Build] with [Tool] ([Qualifier])`
**Use when:** Browsable content, tool roundups, feature highlights.

### B3: "My Complete System" / Anti-Hype
**Formula:** `My COMPLETE [Workflow/System] to [Outcome] (No Fluff)`
**Use when:** Showing your actual production setup. Positions against hype creators.

## AVOID THESE (Consistently underperform)

- Generic motivational/career advice
- Pure opinion without builds
- Fear content without actionable solution
- Live workshop replays without strong repackaging
- Questions as titles — 0.75x average
- "You/Your" direct address — 0.64x average
- Generic "How to" without qualifier — 0.67x average

---

## FREEFORM CURIOSITY (REQUIRED — One of Three Titles Must Be This)

Pattern-based titles are predictable. One title must be a simple, punchy statement that creates pure curiosity. No brackets, no formulas — something you'd say to a friend.

**Good freeform examples:**
- "Nobody is talking about this"
- "I was wrong about [topic]"
- "This changes everything about [topic]"
- "The real reason your [thing] fails"
- "[Topic] has a massive problem"
- "[Tool] isn't what you think"

**Test:** "Would I actually say this in conversation?" If yes, it passes.

## CONTENT-TITLE ALIGNMENT

Before generating titles, verify:

### Step 1: What does the video actually deliver?
- What transformation/outcome does the viewer get?
- What specific problem does this solve?
- What is the viewer able to DO after watching?

### Step 2: Pattern eligibility
| Pattern | ONLY use when video... |
|---------|------------------------|
| S1 Full Course | Is 30+ minutes, covers topic comprehensively |
| S2 Contrarian | Has genuine counter-take with evidence |
| S3 Breaking News | Covers something that happened in last 48 hours |
| A1 Superlative | Can demonstrate dramatic outperformance |
| A2 Money Anchor | Shows deliverable with real client value |
| A3 Exclusivity | Reveals non-obvious insight experienced people miss |
| A4 Honest Review | Tests/evaluates a hyped tool or approach |

### Step 3: Validation
For EACH title, verify:
- Does the video ACTUALLY deliver on this promise?
- Would a viewer feel misled after watching?
- Does the pattern match the content type?

REJECT titles that fail these checks.

## ELEMENT MULTIPLIERS

**Boost performance:**
- First person ("I", "My"): 1.4x
- Specific dollar amount ($10K not "money"): 1.3x
- Numbers (3, 5, 7 — odd preferred): 1.36x
- Current year: 1.13x
- Duration in title (4 HOURS, 28 Minutes): 1.5x
- Power words (FULL COURSE, Destroys, INSANE): 1.36x
- Tool name (specific tool): 1.1x
- Parenthetical hook: standard (table stakes, use one)
- ALL CAPS on 1-2 key words: 1.2x

**Avoid (underperform):**
- Questions: 0.75x
- "You/Your" direct address: 0.64x
- Generic "How to" without qualifier: 0.67x
- Negative framing without alternative: 0.58x

**Optimal length:** 7-12 words, 50-70 characters

---

## YOUR TITLE STRATEGY

Your competitive advantage is the **honest practitioner** angle. Three principles:

1. **Lead with experience, not hype.** "I Let [Tool] Do X for a Week" > "[Tool] Is INSANE"
2. **Second-click, not first-click.** When competitors hype something, you're the "what actually happened" follow-up.
3. **At least one title per video uses A4 (Honest Review) energy.** Even if you pick a different pattern, the voice should be yours.
