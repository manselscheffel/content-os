# Video Analysis — Single Competitor Video

You are analyzing a single competitor YouTube video to extract actionable intelligence for creating a superior response video.

## Input
- Video title, channel handle, view count, publish date
- Full transcript (or title + description if transcript unavailable)
- Competitor's known weaknesses and strategic profile

## Analysis Required

### 1. Topic Summary
- What is this video fundamentally about? (1-2 sentences)
- What specific problem does it claim to solve?
- What transformation does it promise the viewer?

### 2. Key Claims Extraction
List every significant claim, piece of advice, or recommendation:
- What tools/methods do they recommend?
- What results do they claim?
- What methodology do they show vs. just talk about?
- Did they DEMONSTRATE or just CLAIM?

### 3. Methodology Assessment
- What did they actually show working? (demo quality)
- What did they hand-wave or skip over?
- Is this interactive (human-in-the-loop) or autonomous?
- Did they address error handling, edge cases, production readiness?
- Did they mention costs, limitations, or trade-offs?

### 4. Content Pillar Mapping
Map this video to one or more of these pillars:
1. Pricing & Value
2. Sales & Client Acquisition
3. Delivery & Operations
4. Client Retention & Expansion
5. Positioning & Mindset
6. Tools & Technical Credibility
7. Hype with Substance

### 5. Angle Gap Detection
What angles are MISSING from this video?
- Tutorial exists but case study doesn't?
- Beginner content but no advanced follow-up?
- Positive take but no critical analysis?
- Theory but no practical implementation?
- Demo but no production deployment?
- Hype but no cost/reality check?

### 6. Production & Engagement Notes
- Video length and pacing
- Hook quality (first 30 seconds)
- Retention signals (clear structure vs. rambling)
- Call-to-action strategy
- Comment section sentiment (if available)

## Output
Provide your analysis in clear sections with specific quotes from the transcript where relevant. Be precise about what the competitor said vs. what they implied vs. what they left out entirely.
