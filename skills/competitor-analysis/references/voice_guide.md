# YouTube Voice Guide

> **Purpose:** Define how to write talking points for YouTube scripts. Specific to YouTube's conversational, educational format.
>
> **Customize:** Update the voice principles and examples to match your personal style.

---

## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Direct Address - "You" Focus

Speak TO the viewer constantly. Every point connects to their situation.

**Do:**
- "You've been so focused on building that you forgot to decide what business you're actually in."
- "You need to think about it from that perspective."
- "If you're just starting out, I would look at this and think..."

**Don't:**
- Generic third-person ("One might consider...")
- Passive observations ("It can be observed that...")
- Lecturer tone ("Students should note...")

---

### 2. The "Uncomfortable Truth" Pattern

Call out uncomfortable realities the audience knows but hasn't articulated. This creates instant recognition and trust.

**Pattern:** `[What they think the problem is] + "That's not the problem." + [What the problem actually is]`

---

### 3. Experience-Based Authority

Ground everything in personal experience. Never theoretical.

**Pattern:** `[Personal experience] + [Specific observation] + [Lesson learned]`

---

### 4. Blunt Honesty with Self-Awareness

Be direct without being arrogant. Acknowledge your own learning process.

---

### 5. Relatable Vocabulary

Use business language but keep it grounded and occasionally irreverent.

**Preferred terms:**
- "Printing money" (not "generating revenue")
- "Gold mine" (not "opportunity")
- "Hamster wheel" (for inefficient processes)
- "Deep end" (for challenging situations)
- "Dumpster fire" (for failing systems)

**Technical terms to humanize:**
- "To use the technical term, it's a dumpster fire."
- "Your system - whatever the hell that means for your use case."

## Vocabulary Rules

### Power Words (Use These)

| Category | Words |
| --- | --- |
| Problems | "bleeding money", "broken", "chaos", "bottleneck", "constraint", "dumpster fire", "nightmare" |
| Solutions | "clarity", "framework", "roadmap", "positioning", "system" |
| Money | "printing money", "gold mine", "recurring revenue", "upsell", "retainer" |
| Action | "embed yourself", "surface opportunities", "stack values", "nail this", "crush it" |
| Stakes | "vital infrastructure", "critical", "massive mistake", "catastrophe" |

### Banned Words (Never Use)

| Word | Replacement |
| --- | --- |
| "delve" | "get into", "dive into", "look at" |
| "landscape" | specific description of what you mean |
| "unlock" | "get", "achieve", "access" |
| "leverage" (as verb) | "use" |
| "optimize" (alone) | "improve [specific thing]" |
| "synergize" | never use this word |
| "game-changer" | describe the specific outcome |
| "utilize" | "use" |
| "impactful" | "effective", "powerful", or describe the impact |
| "learnings" | "lessons", "what I learned" |

## Signature Patterns

### The "But Here's the Problem" Pivot

After stating a positive or common belief, immediately pivot to the complication.

**Pattern:** `[Exciting thing] + "But here's the problem" / "But here's what's actually happening" + [The reality]`

---

### The "Let Me Show You" Promise

After identifying the problem, promise the solution with specifics.

**Pattern:** `Today I'll/I'm going to [verb] + [specific framework/method] + [that achieves outcome]`

---

### The Myth-Busting Structure

List common beliefs, then systematically destroy them with reality.

**Pattern:** `[List the myths] + "They're true, but not true" + [Break down each one]`

---

### The Vivid Analogy

Use unexpected comparisons to make abstract concepts concrete and memorable.

---

### The Framework Introduction

When introducing a numbered system, follow this pattern:

**Pattern:** `[Name the framework] + [List parts] + "It's simple but powerful" + [Explain benefits]`

## Emotional Range

### Calibrated Intensity Scale

| Level | When to Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Conversational | Main teaching, explanations | "So the delivery process for this is pretty straightforward." |
| Emphatic | Key insights, important points | "That's super important to understand." |
| Warning | Cautionary points, mistakes to avoid | "If you don't set expectations properly up front, you're going to get lost in the weeds." |
| Blunt | Myth-busting, calling out BS | "Build once, run forever? Lies." / "That's a massive problem." |

---

## What This Voice Is NOT

| Avoid | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Salesy | Never pitch directly; let value speak |
| Theoretical | Always ground in experience |
| Preachy | "You" as collaboration, not lecture |
| Over-polished | Slight imperfections are authentic |
| Desperate | Confident without being arrogant |
| Guru-like | You're not on a mountain; you learned the hard way |
| Hype-y | Call out hype, don't create it |

---

## Quick Voice Check

Before finalizing talking points, verify:

1. **Is it direct?** Speaking TO viewer, not ABOUT a topic
2. **Is it grounded?** Real experience, not theory
3. **Is it specific?** Concrete examples, not vague advice
4. **Is it honest?** Including uncomfortable truths
5. **Does it sound human?** Not corporate, not guru

**Final test:** Read it out loud. Does it sound like something you'd say in a conversation, not a presentation?
