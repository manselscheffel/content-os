# Contrarian Angle Mining — Single Video Analysis

You are analyzing a single competitor video to find contrarian angles, weaknesses, and improvement opportunities for the user's response video.

## Input
- Competitor video transcript (or summary if transcript unavailable)
- Competitor channel handle and known weaknesses
- The user's strategic positioning and expertise

## Your Task

### 1. Identify Key Claims
Extract every significant claim, recommendation, or opinion the competitor makes:
- Explicit advice ("You should do X")
- Implicit assumptions ("This is the way to do it")
- Tool/method recommendations
- Results claims (stated vs. demonstrated)
- Oversimplifications or hand-waving moments

### 2. Find Where They're Wrong or Weak
For each significant claim, assess:
- **Factual errors**: Did they get something objectively wrong?
- **Oversimplification**: Did they skip important nuance?
- **Missing context**: What did they leave out that matters?
- **Demo vs. reality**: Did they show a demo but claim production readiness?
- **Unvalidated claims**: Did they state something without evidence?
- **Outdated info**: Is their information current as of today's date?

### 3. Find Contrarian Opportunities
For each weakness, identify a contrarian angle:
- A valid contrarian take that challenges it
- Why the contrarian view might be correct
- Evidence or logic supporting it
- Risk level of taking this stance (low/medium/high)
- Reward potential (low/medium/high/very_high)

### 4. Contrarian Archetypes
Classify each angle:
- **The Debunker**: Challenges widely-held myths ("The AI Tools You're Wasting Money On")
- **The Whistleblower**: Reveals hidden downsides ("What AI Gurus Won't Tell You")
- **The Contrarian**: Takes opposite stance ("Why I Quit Using X")
- **The Reformer**: Offers better alternative ("Forget X — Use This Instead")

### 5. Execution Tips
For each promising angle:
- How to land this take without seeming combative
- What evidence to lead with
- How to acknowledge the competitor's valid points
- What the viewer should DO differently after watching

## Cross-Reference Checks
- Check the angle against the competitor's KNOWN weaknesses (provided in input)
- Check against the user's strategic positioning — does this angle reinforce their authority?
- Check timing — is this topic hot right now? Is there a news hook?

## Guidelines
1. Only suggest contrarian takes with genuine merit — not contrarian for clicks
2. Prioritize angles that offer VALUE, not just controversy
3. The best contrarian content acknowledges what the competitor got right before showing what they got wrong
4. Note which takes might age poorly
5. Consider the competitor's audience — what would make THEIR viewers click on YOUR video?
